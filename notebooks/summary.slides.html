Emulating Majorana fermions braiding on an Ising chain¶

Usual approach requires to go beyond 1D, here an extra coupling spin will allow to work in 1D.

Model Hamiltonian¶

\begin{equation} H = -J \sum_i \sigma_{z, i}\,\sigma_{z, i+1} - \sum-i h_i\,\sigma_{x, i} - J_m \sigma_{z, m}\,\sigma_{z, m+1}\,S_x \end{equation}

Key ideas:

  • ferromagnetic domain ($h \ll J$) = topological region
  • adiabatic switching of $h_i$ allow to move the domain
  • coupler spin S enable braiding in 1D

Logical basis for 3 spins: \begin{align} |0\rangle = \frac{|\uparrow\uparrow\uparrow\rangle + |\downarrow\downarrow\downarrow\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}\qquad |1\rangle = \frac{|\uparrow\uparrow\uparrow\rangle - |\downarrow\downarrow\downarrow\rangle}{\sqrt{2}} \end{align}

Map physical qubit $|0\rangle$ to $|\uparrow\rangle$

S. Backens et al., Emulating Majorana fermions and their braiding by Ising spin chains. Physical Review B. 96 (2017), doi:10.1103/PhysRevB.96.195402.

Marek Mateusz Narozniak & Tim Byrnes

Implementation¶

  • Suzuki-Trotter decomposition handle $\mathcal{H}$
  • adiabatic switching implemented by incremental updates of $h_i$
  • coupler prepared by applying $S_x(\pi)$, then after first transfer apply $S_y(\theta)$ give a phase gate of angle theta

$\rightarrow$ Braiding studied on $|\uparrow\uparrow\uparrow\rangle$ with $\theta = \pi$

$\Rightarrow$ high computational cost (depth > 60000 gates before optimization) but system quite tolerant to large steps

print(qcirc.draw())
qcirc = QuantumCircuit(qreg)
interaction_hamiltonian(qcirc, qreg, 1, 2, 0.14)
print(qcirc.draw())

Outcome¶

  • chain expansion, chain contraction, chain moving: $|1>$ moved on a chain of 6 with > 90% probability without coupler
  • with coupler reduced probabibility: bad decoupling ?
  • full braiding possible: outcome 70% probability between $|\uparrow\uparrow\uparrow\rangle$ and $|\downarrow\downarrow\downarrow\rangle$ (chain of 8/12 spins 3 sites ferro, specific time evolution parameters)
  • longer topological area do not perform as well, coupler issue

Few evolution parameters tested so we may have been lucky
